Research Experience

1.High-performance fiber-based wearable supercapacitor                                                 06/2020-Present

   Main Participants, Project is ongoing

   The Senior Project, Funded by the Innovation and Entrepreneurship Fund for College students

  • Designed a fiber-based  supercapacitor with light weight, high flexibility and energy density, which can be used as a new wearable energy storage device.

  • Produced fiber electrodes by solution spinning with a hydrogel solution of polyacrylate and PEDOT:PSS mixture, twined and twisted two fiber electrodes together to form a two-ply type supercapacitor with a gel polymer electrolyte between them.

​

2. Programmable Multi-stimuli-responsive and Multimodal Polymer Actuator Based on a Designed            Energy Transduction Network                                                                                             01/2021-08/2021

    Main Participants, Project completed, Paper under review.

  • Assembled an actuator using the structure compatibility and function complementarity of three synthetic responsive polymers: polyaniline (PANi), poly(N-isopropylacrylamide) (PNIPAm) and polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS).

  • Programmed the actuator's deformation with sequential stimuli since the actuator can perform complex deformations in response to different stimuli.

​

3. Multifunctional Hydrogel Conductive Optical Fiber with High Stretchability, Anti-freezing and Long-      lasting Water Retention                                                                                                        09/2019-12/2020

    Leader, Project completed, Paper to be submitted.

    Funded by the Innovation and Entrepreneurship Fund for College students

  • Designed an ion-doped polyacrylamide hydrogel optical fiber in a core/clad step index structure that is produced continuously by a simple draw-spinning process.

  • The fibers exhibit a unique stretchability (strain: 1000%) and tensile strength (24 MPa), high electrical conductivity (3.23 S/m), anti-freezing capability (< -90°C) and good light-guiding performance.
